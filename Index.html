CHICKEN
The chicken (Gallus gallus domesticus), a subspecies of the red junglefowl, is a type of domesticated fowl, originally from Asia. Rooster or cock is a term for an adult male bird. A younger male may be called a cockerel; a male that has been castrated is a capon. The adult female bird is called a hen. "Chicken" was originally a term only for an immature, or at least young, bird, but thanks to its usage on restaurant menus has now become the most common term for the subspecies in general, especially in American English. In older sources common fowl or domestic fowl were typically used for this.

BEEF
The cow, in the case of the female; or bull, in the case of the male (Bos primigenius taurus), is an artiodactyl mammal of the bovidae family. The scientific name is the one assigned to the European and North Asian domestic vaccinated animal 1, a group of domestic bovids descended from the subspecies of Eurasian wild aurochs known as Bos primigenius primigenius; while Bos primigenius indicus is called the zebu and other domestic bovine breeds from the same trunk, and descendants of the subspecies of wild aurochs from Southeast Asia, called Bos primigenius namadicus. It is a large ruminant mammal with a robust body, with a height of about 120-150 cm and an average weight of 600-800 kg.
